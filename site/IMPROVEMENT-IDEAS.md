## 1. Spiritual Tone & Content

### Problem
The language reads like an event listing rather than a heartfelt invitation to transformation. It is functional but lacks the devotional warmth (madhura bhava) of the Ananda Marga tradition.

### Suggestions

- **Hero tagline**: "Yoga Retreat in the Baltics" is geographically descriptive but spiritually flat. Add a soul-level hook: *"Come home to your Self"* or *"Days of silence, practice, and inner discovery"*
- **Reframe benefits positively**: "Forget about the daily bustle" focuses on what you leave behind. Better: *"Immerse yourself fully in sadhana, in a setting designed for inner silence"*
- **Warm up teacher bios**: Currently read like CVs. Add a sentence about each teacher's *presence* and teaching style. What does it feel like to learn from them?
- **Use specific, visceral language**: Instead of "feel the connection with your true nature," try *"Experience the stillness of deep meditation in collective practice, guided by monks who have devoted their lives to the path"*
- **Sprinkle poetic moments**: The page should carry some of the devotional sweetness of the tradition. A retreat page for an AM event should not feel interchangeable with any generic yoga event.

## 2. Content Gaps & Glossary

### Problem
Many Ananda Marga-specific terms are used without explanation, creating a barrier for newcomers.

### Terms that need explanation (inline or in FAQ)

| Term | What it means | Priority |
|------|--------------|----------|
| **Personal lesson** | Receiving a meditation technique (mantra initiation) personally from an acharya. *This is arguably the single most valuable offering of the retreat and deserves its own section.* | Critical |
| **Prabhat Samgiita** | "Songs of the New Dawn" — 5,018 devotional songs composed by Shrii Shrii Anandamurti | High |
| **Kiirtana** | Collective devotional chanting and dancing that uplifts the mind | High |
| **Panchajanya** | Pre-dawn collective meditation practice (the most spiritually potent hours) | High |
| **Kaoshiki & Tandava** | Yogic dances with profound physical and psycho-spiritual benefits | High |
| **Sadhana Shivir** | Extended intensive meditation session — one of the most powerful retreat experiences | High |
| **Manuvrata** | Observing silence — turning the mind inward, conserving mental energy | Medium |
| **(20/20), (30/30)** | Minutes of kiirtana followed by minutes of silent meditation | Medium |
| **Sattvic** | Pure, harmonious, and conducive to spiritual practice | Medium |
| **Satsanga** | Gathering with fellow seekers | Low |

### Missing practical information

- **Transport**: How to get there from Vilnius airport? Parking? Bus?
- **Accommodation type**: Shared rooms? Dormitory? Private rooms available?
- **Facilities**: Bathrooms, WiFi policy, bed linens provided?
- **Diet details**: Strictly lacto-vegetarian? Onion/garlic? Allergy accommodations?
- **Language of instruction**: Critical for an international audience
- **What "experienced practitioners" means**: Do you need mantra initiation for Part 2?
- **Marg Retreat schedule**: Part 2 (May 1-4) has zero schedule information

## 3. Emotional Journey & Page Flow

### Current flow
Hero (dates/CTA) -> Benefits -> Teachers -> Activities -> Schedule -> What to Bring -> Location -> Pricing -> Footer

### Problem
The page jumps too quickly from "here is an event" to logistics. No emotional warm-up phase builds desire before presenting practical details.

### Suggested improvements

1. **After Hero, add a "vision" moment**: A brief evocative paragraph or quote painting the retreat experience — the pre-dawn silence, collective kiirtana, the Lithuanian forest at sunrise. *Create longing before logistics.*
2. **Add a "Typical Day" summary**: Before the detailed day-by-day schedule, show a simple overview (wake at 4:30, morning sadhana, asanas, breakfast, lecture, meditation, lunch, satsanga, evening meditation, rest). The full schedule can remain in accordions.
3. **Move "What to Bring" below Pricing**: It interrupts the decision-making flow. A visitor still deciding whether to register doesn't need packing instructions yet.
4. **Add testimonials between Location and Pricing**: This is the emotional tipping point that moves someone from "interested" to "registering."
5. **End with inspiration, not just a button**: After the Register CTA, add a closing quote or line that reinforces the transformative promise.

### Suggested new flow
```
Hero
  -> Vision / Quote (new)
  -> "Retreat Is a Chance to" (improved)
  -> Teachers
  -> Activities
  -> "A Typical Day" (new)
  -> Detailed Schedule (accordion)
  -> Location (expanded)
  -> Testimonials (new)
  -> Pricing
  -> FAQ (new)
  -> "What to Bring" (moved)
  -> Final CTA + closing quote
  -> Footer
```

## 4. Hero Section

### Critical issues

- **Plain CSS gradient with no imagery** — missed opportunity for a yoga retreat. The page has strong hero candidates: `dada-hero.jpeg` (Dada speaking) and `activity-5.png` (gong meditation, visually stunning). Neither is used.
- **No hero image of the actual location or retreat experience**. Visitors want to see themselves in the setting.
- **Dates presented as plain text** — for an event page, dates should be prominent, scannable visual elements.
- **No countdown timer** for early-bird deadline or retreat start.
- **No video background** — even a 10-second ambient loop (people meditating, nature, sunrise) would dramatically elevate emotional impact.

### Suggestions
- Use a full-bleed background image with dark overlay for text legibility
- Consider a styled date badge or horizontal date-range bar
- Replace generic "Scroll" text with "Discover the journey"
- Add a short benefit-driven subheadline: *"7 days of guided meditation, asana practice, and spiritual teachings with monks who have 25+ years of experience"*

## 8. Pricing & CTA

### Critical issues

- **What is included?** Price cards show amounts but not inclusions. Add: "Includes: accommodation, all vegetarian meals, all teachings, personal meditation instruction."
- **Payment method unclear.** How does one pay? Bank transfer? Card? After form?
- **Google Forms breaks the experience.** Immediately signals "informal operation" and loses brand trust. At minimum embed on-page or use a modal.
- **"Price List" is transactional language.** Consider "Reserve Your Place" or "Begin Your Journey"
- **The 10 EUR savings on the combined package (1.6%) is not compelling.** Either increase the discount or reframe around experience value ("7 full days of immersion")
- **No urgency elements.** No countdown, no "X spots remaining," no visual emphasis on early-bird deadline
- **No scarcity signal.** "Limited to 30 participants" or similar creates genuine urgency if true.

### Suggestions
- Add a clear inclusions list under each price card
- Add cancellation/refund policy near pricing
- Add secondary CTA: "Have questions? Message us on Telegram" next to Register
- Add microcopy under button: "Takes 2 minutes. No payment required to reserve."
- Consider meaningful bundle discount (50-80 EUR savings, not 10)

## 10. FAQ Section (New)

**For newcomers:**
- Do I need meditation experience to attend?
- What if I have never been to a retreat before?
- Is this religious?
- What if I can't sit in lotus position?
- What does "personal lesson of meditation" mean?

- What language will the retreat be conducted in?
- How do I get to the venue? Is there airport pickup?
- What are the sleeping arrangements? Private rooms?
- What food is provided? Can you accommodate allergies?
- Is there WiFi? What is the phone policy?
- Can I arrive late or leave early?
- Are children welcome?

**Financial:**
- What is the cancellation/refund policy?
- Can I pay in installments?
- How and when do I pay?
- What is included in the price?

**For experienced practitioners:**
- What makes the Marg Retreat different from Part 1?
- Do I need existing initiation for Part 2?
- Will there be higher lesson review?

## 17. Accessibility (WCAG)

### Color contrast failures (CRITICAL)
- **CTA button**: Terracotta (#C4654A) with white text = ~3.5:1 contrast. **Fails WCAG AA** (needs 4.5:1). Darken to at least #A8503A.
- **Sage-light on cream**: (#A8C4B0 on #F5F0E8) = ~1.7:1. Fails AA. Used for hero kicker text.
- **"Best Value" badge**: 0.65rem text (~10.4px) below recommended 12px minimum.

### Missing features
- No skip-to-content link for screen readers
- No focus styles defined (CSS resets remove defaults without replacements)
- Mobile menu close button has no `aria-label`
- Schedule accordion buttons lack `aria-expanded` state
- No `prefers-reduced-motion` media query — animations force-play
- Lotus icons have `alt=""` but carry meaning — need descriptive alt text
- Copyright text at 0.75rem is too small

## 24. Newcomers vs. Experienced Practitioners

### Problem
The page attempts to serve both audiences but fails both partially.

### For newcomers
- Jargon barrier is high (see Glossary section above)
- No "Is this for me?" reassurance section
- No explanation of what "experienced" means for Part 2
- The 4:30 AM wake-up and dense schedule may intimidate — needs framing: *"Don't worry if you've never woken this early — the collective energy of the group and the beauty of the pre-dawn hours make it one of the most cherished parts of the experience"*

### For experienced practitioners (margis)
- The "Marg Retreat" card gives almost zero information about what makes it different
- No mention of specific advanced practices: longer sadhana, akhanda kiirtana, higher lesson review, dharma chakra
- An experienced margi sees a generic retreat with a mysterious "Part 2"

### Suggestions
- **"Two retreats, one journey"** section explaining both parts clearly
- **"First time?"** callout or FAQ addressing newcomer concerns
- **Expanded Marg Retreat description** with what the advanced part offers
- **Clear statement**: "Part 2 is for those who have received personal mantra initiation (personal lesson)"
